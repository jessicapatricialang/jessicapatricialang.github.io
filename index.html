I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Zurich. My research revolves around the ways democratic societies make sense of environmental degradation such as climate change or biodiversity loss and develop a hegemonic understanding of legitimate ways to address them. In my current project, I explore how different human-nature value orientations shape decision-making processes around Alpine renewable energy projects. Additionally, I serve as the Chair of the ECPR Standing Group on Participation and Mobilisation.

I hold a PhD in Political and Social Sciences from the European University Institute in Florence, Italy. In my dissertation, I assessed the capacity of social movement organisations to initiate changes in the discursive field of the environment and examined how and why they influence public opinion and political behaviour. During my PhD, I was a visiting researcher at the WZB Berlin Social Science Center, the University of Zurich, and the University of Gothenburg. On this website, you can find out about my research, publications, teaching, and CV. Feel free to get in touch at jessica.lang[at]ipz.uzh.ch.
